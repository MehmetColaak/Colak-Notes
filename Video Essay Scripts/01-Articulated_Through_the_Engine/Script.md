An internal combustion engine typically sounds like a rising and falling pitch tone due to faster and slower ignition events happened in the piston chambers. This changing pitch tone also affected by transmission because clutch mechanism separates the engine's crankshaft from the transmission and effecting the pitch of the sound with quick dips. There are some phrases or let's say stingers I observe in the engine play vocabulary.

**RPM Rise:** This may resemble a Crescendo or one-to-one purpose translated a "riser". In my perception it creates an anticipation, an excitement with some cognitive questions about where it is going to stop. Because you already know the direction. The feeling is familiar you are on a aural platform that goes up, up and up.

**RPM Fall:** Similar to the rpm rise resemblance to the Crescendo we can easily label this as Decrescendo in musical vocabulary of engine play.

**Gear Shift:** This is one of the few rhythmic elements of this instrument. Every gear change means clutch separated from the crankshaft, and throttle is off from the engine so slight RPM dip and eventually rise or fall according to throttle movement. This sounds like stingers that perceived as edges of musical phrases.

**Rev Limiter:** In my taste this is most expressive sound bit of an engine that also felt like a rhythmic element. The limiter prevents to go faster above certain speed of revolutions and it sound like this screechy, stuttering motion.
